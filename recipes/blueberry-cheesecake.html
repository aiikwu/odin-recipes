Blueberry Cheesecake

Description

This creamy, rich cheesecake is flavored with a hint of lemon zest and topped with a luscious blueberry sauce. The smooth, tangy filling sits on a classic graham cracker crust, making it the perfect dessert for any occasion. Whether you're celebrating a special event or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this blueberry cheesecake is sure to impress!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups (180g) graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons (85g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 24 oz (680g) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup (120ml) heavy cream
  • 2 cups (300g) fresh or frozen blueberries
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Steps

  1. Prepare the Crust:
    • Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
    • In a medium bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, ¼ cup sugar, and melted butter. Mix until evenly coated.
    • Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch (23cm) springform pan.
    • Bake for 10 minutes, then let it cool while preparing the filling.
  2. Make the Cheesecake Filling:
    • In a large mixing bowl, rub the lemon zest into 1 cup sugar until fragrant.
    • Add softened cream cheese and beat with an electric mixer until smooth.
    • Mix in vanilla extract.
    • Add eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed after each addition. Do not overmix.
    • Add heavy cream and mix until combined.
  3. Bake the Cheesecake:
    • Pour the cheesecake filling over the prepared crust.
    • Wrap the outside of the springform pan with aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in.
    • Place the pan inside a large roasting pan and fill the roasting pan with about 1 inch of hot water (water bath).
    • Bake for 55-65 minutes, until edges are set but the center slightly jiggles.
    • Turn off the oven, crack the door open, and leave the cheesecake inside for 1 hour.
    • Let it cool completely, then refrigerate for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  4. Make the Blueberry Topping:
    • In a small saucepan, combine blueberries, ½ cup sugar, and lemon juice over medium heat.
    • Mix cornstarch with water, then add to the saucepan.
    • Stir and cook until thickened (5-7 minutes).
    • Let cool completely before spreading over the chilled cheesecake.
  5. Serve and Enjoy!
    • Release the cheesecake from the springform pan and transfer to a serving plate.
    • Spoon blueberry topping over the cheesecake.
    • Slice and enjoy!
